# Before/After Examples: Transformations by Category

Extensive collection of before/after examples showing how to transform AI-generated content into authentic human writing.

---

## Blog Posts & Articles

### Example 1: Tech Tutorial Introduction

**AI Version:**
"In the ever-evolving digital landscape, it is crucial to master the comprehensive
nature of modern web development. This multifaceted realm encompasses various critical
technologies that developers must meticulously consider. Moreover, the ecosystem
continues to burgeon with innovative solutions. This tutorial will delve into the
intricacies of React, empowering you to harness its potential for crafting exemplary
user interfaces."

**Human Revision:**
"I've been building web apps for ten years. When React first came out, I thought it
was overcomplicated garbage. I was wrong. After three years of resisting, I finally
gave it a shot—and now I can't imagine building UIs any other way. This tutorial
will show you why React clicked for me, and how it can work for you too. Not the
fluffy stuff—the actual patterns I use every day."

**Changes made:**
- Removed: ever-evolving digital landscape, crucial, comprehensive, multifaceted realm, meticulously, moreover, burgeon, delve, intricacies, harness, exemplary (11 AI markers)
- Added: Personal experience (ten years), genuine opinion (thought it was garbage), specific timeline (three years), contractions (I've, can't), conversational tone
- Voice: Honest about initial resistance, shares transformation
- Structure: Varied sentence lengths (4 words to 32 words)
- Word count: AI (67 words) vs Human (74 words)—human is more meaningful despite similar length

### Example 2: Opinion Piece on Remote Work

**AI Version:**
"Remote work has garnered significant attention in recent times. It is important to
note that this paradigm shift presents both notable opportunities and substantial
challenges for organizations. Furthermore, the landscape of work continues to evolve

figuring this out—the ones that treat remote work as just 'office work but on Zoom'
are failing. The ones that redesign how work happens? They're winning."

**Changes made:**
- Removed: garnered significant attention, important to note, paradigm shift, notable opportunities, substantial challenges, furthermore, landscape, holistic, consequently, strategically consider, optimize, realm (12 AI markers)
- Added: Personal details (commute, kids, stress), honest assessment (isolation), specific observation (thumbs up on Slack), clear opinion, contractions
- Voice: Vulnerable about downsides, takes clear position
- Structure: Conversational opening, varied sentence lengths
- Word count: AI (73 words) vs Human (79 words)

## Academic Writing

### Example 16: Research Paper Abstract

**AI Version:**
"This research meticulously examines the multifaceted relationship between remote work
policies and employee productivity. By leveraging a comprehensive dataset encompassing
diverse industries, this study elucidates the nuanced impacts of distributed work
arrangements. The findings underscore the significance of context-specific approaches
to organizational policy formulation. Consequently, this research contributes valuable
insights to the burgeoning discourse on workplace transformation."

a 12% decline. These findings suggest that one-size-fits-all remote work policies miss
important nuances. The most effective policies account for role differences."

**Changes made:**
- Removed: meticulously examines, multifaceted, leveraging, comprehensive, encompassing diverse, elucidates, nuanced, underscore, significance, burgeoning, discourse (11 AI markers)
- Added: Specific numbers (500 companies, 10,000 employees), concrete findings (18% increase, 12% decrease), clear claim, specific recommendation
- Tone: Academic but clear, confident claims
- Structure: Problem, method, findings, implications
- Word count: AI (76 words) vs Human (83 words)

### Example 17: Literature Review

**AI Version:**
"Scholars have extensively documented the intricate relationship between social media
usage and mental health outcomes. Furthermore, research suggests a multifaceted impact
that varies across demographic groups. However, the literature reveals notable gaps in
understanding long-term effects. Consequently, future research must adopt a holistic
approach to comprehensively address this complex phenomenon."

**Human Revision:**
"Research on social media and mental health shows mixed results. Some studies find
negative effects, particularly among adolescents—Twenge et al. (2018) document rising

effects or even benefits for certain groups, especially those using social media to
maintain connections. The literature has a glaring gap: we don't have good longitudinal
data. Most studies track participants for weeks or months, not years. Without
longitudinal research, we can't distinguish correlation from causation."

**Changes made:**
- Removed: extensively documented, intricate, furthermore, multifaceted, however, notable gaps, consequently, must adopt a holistic approach, comprehensively, complex phenomenon (10 AI markers)
- Added: Specific citation (Twenge et al., 2018), honest assessment (mixed results), clear gap identification (longitudinal data), scholarly tone
- Tone: Academic but not hedging, specific critique
- Structure: Summary of findings, identification of gap
- Word count: AI (63 words) vs Human (92 words)

### Example 18: Discussion Section

**AI Version:**
"The results of this study align with prior research in significant ways. It is
noteworthy that the observed effects were robust across multiple contexts. However,
certain limitations should be carefully considered. Specifically, the sample size,
while adequate for initial analysis, may not capture the full complexity of the
phenomenon under investigation. Future research should endeavor to replicate these
findings in more diverse settings."

**Human Revision:**
"Our findings support previous work on team dynamics—specifically, that psychological
safety predicts performance (Edmondson, 1999). However, the effect size we observed
(d=0.68) is larger than most prior studies report. This might be due to our focus on
remote teams, where safety mechanisms differ from in-person settings.

Limitations are straightforward: our sample skewed toward tech companies, and we
relied on self-reported performance. Future studies should use objective performance
metrics and include more industries. Replication is particularly important given our
larger-than-expected effect sizes."

**Changes made:**
- Removed: align with prior research in significant ways, noteworthy, robust, carefully considered, specifically, endeavor to, phenomenon under investigation (8 AI markers)
- Added: Specific citation (Edmondson, 1999), concrete statistics (d=0.68), honest speculation (might be due to), clear limitations, specific future directions
- Tone: Scholarly, confident where appropriate, honest about limitations
- Structure: Connects to prior work, explains findings, acknowledges limitations
- Word count: AI (70 words) vs Human (97 words)

## Summary of Common Patterns

### What Changes Across All Examples

**Removed (AI markers):**
- delve, tapestry, symphony, realm, comprehensive, holistic
- leverage, harness, unlock, unleash, orchestrate, elevate, foster
- moreover, furthermore, consequently, therefore
- important to note, worth noting
- significant, important, effective, essential (when used vaguely)

**Added (human elements):**
- Specific numbers, dates, names
- Personal examples and stories
- Honest emotions and reactions
- Clear opinions and positions
- Conversational phrasing and contractions
- Concrete details over abstractions

**Structural changes:**
- Varied sentence lengths
- Different sentence openings
- Some fragments for emphasis
- Natural flow without forced transitions
- Context-appropriate formatting
